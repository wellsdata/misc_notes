RICHESON'S CELLMATE
GETS TWELVE YEARS.
Butts, the Negro, Pleads Guilty to
Charge of Manslaughter.
Boston, May 27.- Harry H. Butts,
the negro who shot and killed Robert
Wilson in this city last June, was sentenced
to not less than 12 nor more
than 14 years in state prison by Judge
Crosby in the superior court today.
Butts was indicted for murder in the
second degree, but was allowed to
plead guilty to manslaughter. Butts
claimed that Wilson had ruined
his
home.
The negro was the companion
of
Clarence V. T. Richeson while the lat-
ter was imprisoned in the
street jail before his execution. Charles
