Seven Colored Laborers Victims
Diaz, Mexico, May 23.- There is
great excitement in the town of San
Dialo, ten miles from here, over the
lynching of seven colored laborers
on the Mexican Central Railway.
Jose Santo, a Spanish negro, attempted
to assault the wife of Senoring
Duply, a ranch owner, and, escaping
from the plantation, was track-
ed by bloodhounds and in company
with nine others were captured in a
hut on the river bank opposite
Eagle Pass. The regulators did
not attempt to prove the guilt of any
of the negroes, but hanged three of
them and shot four more who were
attempting to escape. The officials
are swearing in deputies, as race
trouble is threatened in the colored
quarter.
