A NEW mode of" removal" has come
to light in West Virginia. Charles
West, of Fayette County, was brutally
murdered by a worthless negro. The
negro was promptly lynched but before
he could be strangled he succeeded in
making a statement that caused an in-
vestigation, which developed that he
was hired to kill West by some of the
very men who lynched him. West
having become obnoxious to them
through political reasons. His cousin
broke down under examination, confessing
the whole plot and it is likely a
few quiet hangings may, follow.
